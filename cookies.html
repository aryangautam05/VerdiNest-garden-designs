Introduction to Cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites to help them remember information about your visit. They enhance your browsing experience by enabling websites to function more efficiently and deliver personalized content.

This Cookies Policy explains which cookies we use, why we use them, and your rights to control them when visiting VerdiNest Garden Designs.

The following information aims to provide a detailed understanding of cookies and their importance in modern web browsing. We have expanded this content to cover various facets related to cookies, data privacy, consent management, technological aspects, and best practices, ensuring transparency for our users.

Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

These are necessary for the basic operation of our website. They enable you to navigate the site and use its features securely. Without essential cookies, some parts of the website may not work properly.

Performance Cookies

These cookies collect anonymous data about how visitors use our website, such as page visits and loading times. This helps us improve website performance and fix issues to provide a smooth user experience.

Functionality Cookies

Functionality cookies remember your preferences, such as language selection or login details, to enhance user convenience and allow a personalized experience tailored to your needs.

Advertising and Targeting Cookies

These cookies track your browsing habits to show you relevant advertisements and measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns. They enable us to provide offers and ads that are most meaningful to you.

How We Use Cookies

Cookies allow us to personalize your experience and analyze traffic patterns. We also use cookies to remember your consent preferences and manage site functionality seamlessly.

Our analytics cookies help us understand the popularity of our pages and identify usage trends to enhance site content and layout. Marketing cookies help optimize campaigns and prevent showing you irrelevant advertising.

Our cookie management approach respects your choices and privacy, dynamically adjusting cookie behavior when consent is declined or withdrawn.

Third-Party Cookies

Our website may include cookies from third-party services like Google Analytics, social media platforms, and advertising partners. These cookies are set by external domains and are subject to the respective third-party privacy policies.

We carefully select these partners to ensure their compliance with relevant privacy laws and standards, minimizing risk and protecting your data.

Examples include analytics platforms measuring how users interact with our site, social media widgets enabling sharing or likes, and advertising platforms that deliver personalized ads.

Managing Cookies

You are in control of cookies. You can configure your browser settings to block or delete cookies. However, disabling some cookies may impact website functionality and your user experience.

Most browsers allow you to control cookies via their preferences or settings panels, providing options to disable cookies entirely, block third-party cookies, or delete cookies selectively.

Additionally, you may use privacy-focused browser extensions or tools designed to manage cookies and trackers for greater control and transparency.

We recommend reviewing your cookie settings periodically to ensure they reflect your preferences accurately.

Cookie Lifespan

Cookies have varied lifespans based on their function:

  • Session cookies: Last until browser is closed
  • Persistent cookies: Remain for minutes to years, often up to 2 years
  • Flash cookies and other local storage items: May persist longer and have alternative deletion methods

When cookies expire, the website may request them again to restore functionality or preferences.

Cookies Glossary

Cookie: A small text file stored on your device by a web browser.

First-party cookie: A cookie set by the website you are visiting.

Third-party cookie: A cookie set by a domain other than the website you are visiting.

Session cookie: A cookie that is deleted when you close your browser.

Persistent cookie: A cookie that remains on your device after you close your browser.
